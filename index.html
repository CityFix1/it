🫀 What is rPPG? (remote Photoplethysmography)

rPPG is a contactless technology that measures your heart rate by analyzing subtle color changes in your skin caused by blood flow. This is the same technology used in smartwatches, but without any physical contact!

📐 How rPPG Works - Step by Step

1. 📷 Camera Captures Your Face
The camera captures your face in real-time. The region of interest is your forehead or cheeks - areas with good blood perfusion.
2. 🎨 Extract Skin Color Signal
The system extracts the average RGB color values from the skin region every frame. This creates a continuous color signal over time.
3. ❤️ Blood Volume Pulse
When your heart contracts (systole), blood volume in your skin increases, making it slightly redder. When it relaxes (diastole), blood volume decreases, making it slightly paler.
4. 📊 Signal Processing
The color signal is filtered to remove noise (movement, lighting changes). The dominant frequency of the remaining signal corresponds to your heart rate.
5. 🔢 Calculate BPM
The frequency is converted to Beats Per Minute (BPM) by multiplying by 60.
Example: 1.2 Hz × 60 = 72 BPM

⚠️ Limitations & Accuracy

rPPG accuracy depends on:
• Good lighting (natural or bright indoor light)
• Minimal movement (stay still for best results)
• Skin visibility (forehead or cheeks must be visible)
• Signal quality (the stronger the color signal, the more accurate the BPM)

📊 Typical accuracy: ±3-5 BPM compared to medical-grade ECG.
